Poha Recipe

Description

Poha is a popular Indian breakfast dish made from flattened rice (poha) that is lightly fried with onions, mustard seeds, curry leaves, and green chilies. It's a quick and nutritious dish, often garnished with peanuts, fresh coriander, and a squeeze of lemon for added flavor. Poha is typically flavored with turmeric, giving it a vibrant yellow color. It's light yet filling, making it an ideal breakfast or snack option. The dish is versatile and can be customized with vegetables like peas, potatoes, or grated coconut.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups flattened rice (poha)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit or chopped
  • 1/4 cup peanuts
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 10-12 curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 1-2 tbsp oil
  • resh coriander and lemon (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Rinse Poha Rinse the flattened rice (poha) in water for a few seconds and drain. Set aside to soften.
  2. Heat Oil:In a pan, heat oil over medium heat.
  3. Add Mustard Seeds: Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Sauté Peanuts: Add peanuts and sauté until they are lightly golden.
  5. Add Onions and Chilies: Stir in curry leaves and sauté for a minute.
  6. Mix Turmeric: Add turmeric powder and salt, mixing well.
  7. Add Poha: Gently add the softened poha and mix everything together.
  8. Cook: Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring gently to combine all flavors.
  9. Garnish: Remove from heat and garnish with fresh coriander and a squeeze of lemon before serving.
